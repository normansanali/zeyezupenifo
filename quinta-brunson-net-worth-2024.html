Early Life and Career Beginnings

Born and raised in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, Quinta Brunson’s journey to stardom began with her passion for comedy and storytelling. She attended Temple University, where she studied Journalism. However, her career took a different turn when she started making and sharing videos on Instagram, which quickly gained popularity.

Breakthrough with BuzzFeed

In 2014, Brunson joined BuzzFeed as a video producer and development partner, where she created and starred in several viral videos. Her series “The Girl Who’s Never Been on a Nice Date” became a massive hit, catapulting her to internet fame.

Transition to Television and Film

After leaving BuzzFeed in 2018, Brunson transitioned to television and film. She starred in the CW’s “iZombie” and HBO’s “A Black Lady Sketch Show,” further establishing her presence in the industry.

Success with “Abbott Elementary”

Brunson’s biggest success came with the creation of the ABC sitcom “Abbott Elementary,” where she serves as the creator, writer, and lead actress. The show has been praised for its humor and representation, significantly contributing to Brunson’s net worth.
