Bernard John “Bernie” Ebbers (born August 27, 1941) is a Canadian businessman who was born in the United States. He was a co-founder of the telecommunications firm WorldCom and served as its chief executive officer for a number of years. Following WorldCom’s fraudulent financial reporting and the resulting loss of US$100 billion to investors, he was found guilty of fraud and conspiracy in 2005, and was sentenced to prison. Until the Madoff Ponzi schemes came to light in 2008, the WorldCom affair was the biggest accounting fraud in the history of the United States of America. He is presently incarcerated in the Oakdale Federal Correctional Complex in Louisiana, where he is serving a 25-year sentence. Among the worst CEOs in American history, according to Portfolio.com and CNBC (which often promoted WorldCom throughout the 1990s), Ebbers was rated the fifth-worst CEO in American history, while Time magazine labelled him the tenth-most corrupt CEO of all time, respectively.
