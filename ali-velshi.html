    Ali Velshi Facts

  • While studying at Queen’s University, he made headlines for organizing protests against Preston Manning and Canada’s Reform Party.
  • He received the Queen’s University Alumni Achievement Award, which is the highest award given to a Queen’s University alumni, in 2010. Additionally, his alma mater also awarded Velshi an honorary doctorate of laws in 2016.
  • Following his graduation, he was awarded a fellowship to the United States Congress from the American Political Science Association in 1996. In this capacity, he worked with the Democratic representative from Indiana, Lee H. Hamilton.
  • While discussing the 2007–2008 financial crisis during an episode of The Colbert Report, comedian Stephen Colbert called Velshi ‘CNN’s business reporter from our hairless, raceless future’.
  • His film credits include Wall Street: Money Never Sleeps (2010), Around the World in 90 Seconds (2006), among many others.
  • In 2005, Velshi hosted 13 hour-long episodes of the reality television show, The Turnaround. For each episode, he traveled across America visiting small business owners who were struggling and helped them connect with high-profile mentors for guidance.
  • His famous moniker the ‘Hairless Prophet of Doom’ or ‘H-POD’ was given to him by The Daily Show host, Jon Stewart.
  • Velshi has published 2 books titled Gimme My Money Back: Your Guide to Beating the Financial Crisis (2009) and How to Speak Money: The Language & Knowledge You Need Now (2011).
  • From September 2001 until December 2004, he hosted several shows for the business news channel CNNfn in New York City. However, after the network shut down, he was reassigned to the United States CNN network.
