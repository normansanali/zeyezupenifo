Beyonc Is All Business Going into 2024 in a Preppy Blazer and Miniskirt

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, July 29, 2024

Just mere hours into 2024 and Beyoncé fans already have a blessing to count, as Queen Bey graced her millions of followers’ timelines with photos of her chic, preppy New Year’s look.

The “Cuff It” songstress channeled both Cher Horowitz and Serena van der Woodsen in a gray, black, and white plaid tweed co-ord composed of a tiny pleated miniskirt and blazer. She wore the blazer open to reveal a slightly sheer white plunging corset top, and paired it with matching strappy, pointed-toe stilettos. Beyoncé further accessorized her sultry, sophisticated look with a small structured purse, white rectangle-framed shades, and—because it wouldn’t be NYE without some bling—a diamond necklace and statement drop earrings. To show it’s all in the details, the singer included some close up shots of her glam, which included shimmery silver eyeshadow to brighten up her inner corner and a glossy muted pink lip, as well as her festively manicured nails, which featured a red Santa Claus hat design. As for hair, the mogul styled her platinum blonde locks in soft, voluminous waves.

In her carousel of images, Beyoncé strikes a pose in front of a blue-and-silver decorated Christmas tree, in an elevator, and in the reflection of a silver ornament, while also including a couple flicks of a shiny silver Christmas tree and a glass ornament with a glittery silver bird inside. In classic, mysterious Beyoncé style, she went sans caption.

The post comes a week after Queen Bey shared several snaps of another bombshell outfit, this time a Canadian tuxedo glammed up to the nines with gems. She wore the stunning denim ensemble by PatBo, which was composed of a light-wash bedazzled jacket, matching jewel-decorated straight-leg jeans, and a crystal-encrusted bra, while flying private to Brazil. She joined her fans in celebrating the country’s premiere of Renaissance: A Film by Beyoncé at a party in Salvador called “Club Renaissance.”
