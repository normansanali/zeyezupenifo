How wide should fascia board be?

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, July 22, 2024

Answer

Replacement fascias are available in a variety of thicknesses depending on the manufacturer, but the rule of thumb is that they should be between 16mm and 25mm thick in order to qualify structurally as a replacement board – our most popular thickness is the 18mm Replacement Fascia.

As a result, what size should the fascia board be is important.

Lumber 1 inch in thickness is used to make the fascia boards. As with the width of rafters, the width of fascia boards is determined by the width of the rafters. However, since roof construction is often done with two by six or two by eight timber, the most usual proportions for fascia boards are one by six or one by eight inches in width.
