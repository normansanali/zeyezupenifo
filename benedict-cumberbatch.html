Benedict Cumberbatch is an English actor, theatre artist, producer, and voice actor who is best known for having been cast as Sherlock Holmes in the series Sherlock since 2010, a role that has won him accolades like Primetime Emmy Award for “Outstanding Lead Actor in a Miniseries or a Movie”, Satellite Award for “Best Actor – Miniseries or Television Film”, and National Television Award for “Best TV Detective”. In addition to that, he has bagged the Laurence Olivier Award for “Best Actor” for Frankenstein and received an Academy Award nomination for “Best Actor” for his role as Alan Turing in The Imitation Game, a 2014 historical drama film. He was included in the annual list of the “100 most influential people in the world” curated by Time magazine in 2014 and was appointed a CBE in the 2015 Birthday Honours for services to the performing arts and to charity.

Education

Cumberbatch started going to boarding school at the age of 8. He studied at Brambletye School in West Sussex and Harrow School, from where he has an art scholarship.

His interest in acting began at Harrows wherein he participated in various Shakespearean works.

After completing school, he volunteered for an English teaching job at a Tibetan Monastery in Darjeeling, India, which lasted for one year.

As soon as he returned, he started pursuing his career in acting by taking Drama classes from the University of Manchester wherein he has a BA degree in Drama and followed it by training from the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art, from where he graduated with an MA in Classical Acting.
