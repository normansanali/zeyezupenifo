Early Life and Career Beginnings

David Choe’s journey to wealth began in Los Angeles, California, where he was born to Korean immigrant parents. His passion for art was evident from a young age, and it became the cornerstone of his career. Choe’s early work was marked by a raw and expressive style, which quickly garnered attention in the art community.

The Rise to Fame

Choe’s rise to fame was not a conventional one. His graffiti and murals, often infused with themes of desire, degradation, and exaltation, caught the eye of both the public and art collectors. His work on the streets transitioned to galleries, where his pieces commanded high prices, contributing significantly to his net worth.

Facebook Mural and Stock Payment

One of the most pivotal moments in Choe’s financial journey was his decision to paint murals for Facebook’s first offices in 2005. Instead of a cash payment, Choe accepted stock options. This choice would later prove to be incredibly lucrative when Facebook went public in 2012, skyrocketing his net worth.

Art Sales and Exhibitions

Choe’s art sales have been a substantial source of his income. His exhibitions often sell out, with individual pieces fetching six to seven figures. The demand for his work continues to grow, which in turn, positively impacts his net worth.
