Sophia Bush Biography | Wiki

Sophia Bush is an American actress, activist, director and producer. She is currently a producer and stars as Dr. Samantha “Sam” Griffith in the present medical comedy-drama Good Samaritan. She is also well-known for her philanthropic efforts and social activism, such as her work with the Time’s Up movement and DoSomething.org.

Bush made her film debut in 2002, opposite Ryan Reynolds in National Lampoon’s Van Wilder. She then appeared in a number of television shows, including Nip/Tuck, Sabrina the Teenage Witch, and the HBO film Point of Origin. She was cast as Brooke Davis in the WB television series One Tree Hill in 2003. She became a spokesperson for high-profile brands after becoming well-known.

She has appeared on the covers of numerous popular magazines, including Entertainment Weekly, Lucky, Maxim, Glamour, InStyle, and Zooey Magazine. She’s also had several endorsement deals, including one with Ocean Pacific clothing. Bush and her One Tree Hill co-stars endorsed MasterCard, Kmart, Chevrolet Cobalt, and Cingular Wireless. She directed three episodes of the series, including the ninth and final season’s penultimate episode.

Bush joined the cast of Chicago P.D. in August 2013 as Det. Erin Lindsay. The show debuted on January 8, 2014. After four seasons, she left the show. She announced her departure from the show in December 2018, citing “abusive behavior.” Bush also appeared frequently on the first and third Chicago franchise series, Chicago Fire and Chicago Med.

In 2018, Bush voiced Voyd, an aspiring superhero, in Pixar’s Incredibles 2, the sequel to 2004’s The Incredibles. She played Veronica in four episodes of the first season of Love, Victor on Hulu in 2020. In 2021, she launched the Drama Queens podcast with One Tree Hill co-stars Hilarie Burton and Bethany Joy Lenz.

Sophia Bush Family

Bush was born and raised by her parents in Pasadena, California. She has no siblings as she is the only child of Maureen Searson and Charles William Bush. Sophia graduated from Westridge School for Girls in 2000 where she also played volleyball for the school team.

Sophia Bush and Chad Michael Murray

Bush was engaged to her co-star Chad Michael Murray in May 2004. They married and held their wedding on April 16, 2005, in Santa Monica, California. Five months later, they separated and their divorce was finalized in December 2006.
